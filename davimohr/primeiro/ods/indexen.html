Sustainable Development Goals

What are the SDGs?

 The SDGs refer to the Sustainable Development Goals. The SDGs are a series of 17 goals established by the United Nations in 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. These goals were created to address the social, economic and environmental challenges faced around the world, and aim to promote people's prosperity and well-being, protect the planet and ensure global peace and partnership.

 One of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals has specific targets to be achieved by the year 2030. Some of the main goals include eradicating poverty, promoting gender equality, ensuring quality education, combating climate change, conserving natural resources, promoting health and well-being, among others.

 The SDGs are a call to action for governments, the private sector, civil society and individuals to work together towards a more sustainable and equitable future. Implementing the Sustainable Development Goals requires coordinated efforts at global, national and local levels, and involves a wide range of actors and initiatives around the world. Reflections on Physical Education


 1.What is the importance of public open spaces in promoting physical activity and community health? Discuss how parks, squares, walking trails and other public outdoor areas can positively influence people's exercise habits. What are the physical and mental benefits associated with practicing physical activity in these spaces?

 Public open spaces play a crucial role in promoting physical activity and improving community health. They provide an accessible and inviting environment for people to engage in a variety of physical activities, such as walking, running, cycling, sports games and outdoor exercise. These spaces play a multifaceted role in promoting health and well-being, both physical and mental, and have a significant positive impact in many ways.



  2.What are the design factors that make a public space attractive for physical activity? How can a well-designed space encourage different age groups and fitness levels to get involved in outdoor activities?

  A well-designed public space can play a significant role in encouraging people of different age groups and fitness levels to get involved in outdoor activities. By designing a public space with these factors in mind, it is possible to create an environment that attracts and motivates a wide variety of people to engage in outdoor physical activity. The inclusion of elements that promote social interaction, accessibility and safety will ensure that the space is used effectively by different groups, contributing to a healthier and more active community.

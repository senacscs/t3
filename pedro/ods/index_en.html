The Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE) plays a key role in promoting and monitoring SDG 12 in Brazil. As the body responsible for producing and disseminating statistical, geographical and environmental information, the IBGE contributes to the advancement of indicators related to consumption and production.
With regard to waste management, the IBGE carries out the National Basic Sanitation Survey, which collects data on the collection, treatment and final disposal of solid waste in Brazil. This information is crucial for implementing strategies to reduce, recycle and properly treat waste in order to achieve the goals of SDG 12.

SDG 12, or Sustainable Development Goal 12, is part of the 2030 Agenda formulated by the United Nations to promote global sustainable development. SDG 12 aims to guarantee consumption and production patterns. This goal is key to tackling global challenges related to excessive consumption, waste generation and environmental degradation. Humans use natural resources unsustainably, leading to resource depletion, fuel and biodiversity loss. Sustainable Development Goal 12 aims to reverse this trend and promote consumption and production patterns towards more attractive practices. Achieving this goal requires more efficient use of resources and energy, eliminating waste and promoting recycling. In addition, awareness should be raised about the importance of responsible consumption and consumers should be informed about the most available options on the market.
SDG 12 also aims to encourage industry and companies to adopt more active practices in their operations. This involves promoting energy efficiency, the use of renewable raw materials, fuel reduction and the implementation of responsible environmental management practices.
